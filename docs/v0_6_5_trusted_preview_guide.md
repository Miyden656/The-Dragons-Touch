# The Dragon’s Touch v0.6.5 — First Trusted Preview Guide

## Purpose of this preview

This preview is meant for 1–2 trusted people only. The goal is not to advertise The Dragon’s Touch publicly yet. The goal is to confirm whether someone besides the creator can follow the workflow, understand the reports, and use the generated prompt to get a useful Commander deck review.

v0.6.5 is a philosophy-aware report and prompt guidance checkpoint. It is not the final product, and it is not meant to replace the pilot’s judgment. The tool should help the pilot understand the deck, review possible cuts, protect important cards, and think through replacements.

## What testers should test

Ask testers to run or review a small number of decks, ideally 1–3 decks total.

Good test decks include:

- A normal legal 100-card Commander deck.
- An over-100-card deck or card pool that needs cuts.
- A deck with a clear theme, commander plan, or unusual build-around.
- A deck where the pilot already knows what the deck is supposed to do.

The best early test is not “does the tool find the perfect answer?” The best early test is:

- Can the tester follow the workflow?
- Does the report make sense?
- Does the guided prompt ask useful questions?
- Does the final review preserve the pilot’s intent?
- Does the system avoid forcing bad recommendations?

## Recommended tester workflow

1. Give the tester one generated deck report and one generated user-guided prompt.
2. Ask them to paste the user-guided prompt into ChatGPT or their preferred AI tool.
3. Ask them to follow the prompt’s instructions.
4. Ask them to upload or paste the generated deck report when prompted.
5. Ask them to answer the guided sections honestly.
6. Ask them to confirm or correct the final intent summary.
7. Ask them to save the final deck review.
8. Ask them to provide feedback using the questions below.

## What feedback to request

Use these questions for testers:

1. Could you tell what the deck was trying to do after reading the report?
2. Did the guided prompt ask questions in a clear order?
3. Did any section feel confusing, too long, or unnecessary?
4. Did the final intent summary accurately reflect your answers?
5. Did the final recommendations feel useful?
6. Did the tool protect cards that mattered to the deck’s plan?
7. Did the tool suggest reviewing cards that actually felt replaceable?
8. Did the philosophy lens help the review, or did it feel distracting?
9. Did collection-only or collection-preferred wording make sense?
10. What was the most useful part of the process?
11. What was the most frustrating part of the process?
12. Would you use this again after improvements?

## What feedback is not needed yet

For this preview, testers do not need to judge:

- Desktop application design.
- Visual polish.
- Full public release readiness.
- Perfect replacement-card accuracy.
- Competitive/cEDH optimization.
- Whether the tool should be monetized.
- Whether every philosophy persona is perfect.

Those are later concerns.

## What v0.6.5 should demonstrate

v0.6.5 should demonstrate that The Dragon’s Touch can:

- Generate a complete deck report.
- Include the full decklist for AI handoff.
- Include annotated card role notes.
- Identify required cuts versus optional optimization cuts.
- Explain protected cards.
- Show collection candidates without treating them as automatic swaps.
- Generate a user-guided prompt.
- Ask for the deck report before starting the review.
- Move through the review one section at a time.
- Accept numbered answers, short answers, and N/A.
- Let the pilot correct the strategy read.
- Preserve pilot intent over script assumptions.
- Use a philosophy lens as review framing.
- Keep philosophy guidance separate from mechanical scoring.

## Good showcase examples

Current strong examples:

### Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd — Theme / Vibe

This example shows the Theme / Vibe lens working well. The pilot wanted the deck to stay close to Dog/corgi typal while still functioning. The final review corrected the secondary strategy toward ETB Control / Flicker Control, protected theme/function cards, and made collection-only recommendations without abandoning the deck’s identity.

### Vadrik, Astral Archmage — Commander Exploiter

This example shows the Commander Exploiter lens working well. The pilot corrected the secondary strategy from Token Combat / Go-Wide-Go-Tall to Voltron, specifically engine Voltron. The final review protected buyback spells and Vadrik-specific cost-reduction support while challenging cards that did not help the commander’s unique plan.

## How to describe this preview to testers

Use wording like this:

“This is an early trusted preview of a free Commander deck review tool I’m building. It is not a finished product yet. I’m testing whether the report and guided review workflow are understandable and useful. I’m mostly looking for feedback on clarity, usefulness, and whether the final review respects what you want your deck to do.”

## What to avoid promising

Do not promise that v0.6.5 will:

- Build perfect decks.
- Always identify the best cuts.
- Always find the best replacement cards.
- Understand every commander perfectly.
- Replace experienced deck-building judgment.
- Automatically optimize for competitive play.
- Be ready for public release.

## Trusted preview success criteria

v0.6.5 is successful if testers say:

- They could follow the report and prompt.
- The workflow made them think clearly about their deck.
- The final review reflected their intent.
- The tool avoided reckless or forced cuts.
- The philosophy lens helped explain recommendations.
- They would want to test a later version.
