# The Dragon’s Touch — Example Deck Reviews

The Dragon’s Touch is a free community Magic: The Gathering Commander deck-building support tool.

It is a local Python-based Commander deck review assistant designed to help players better understand their decks, review possible cuts, identify protected cards, compare strategy fit, and think through replacement options. It does not replace the player, the deckbuilder, or existing deckbuilding websites. Its purpose is to reduce busywork and help the player make better-informed decisions while keeping the final choice in the player’s hands.

## What The Dragon’s Touch Does

The Dragon’s Touch can help review Commander decks by looking at areas such as:

- Commander identity and strategy
- Primary and secondary deck plans
- Cut pressure and optional tuning
- Cards that should be protected from cuts
- Cards that may be off-plan, redundant, low-impact, or replaceable
- Collection-only upgrade paths
- Replacement categories
- Guided review prompts for another AI or human reviewer

The tool is meant to support deckbuilding judgment, not automate it completely.

## AI Transparency

The Dragon’s Touch is being built with heavy AI assistance.

I am not a professional software developer, and I would not be able to build this from scratch without AI helping with code, structure, debugging, documentation, and testing support. I also do not have the money to hire a development team.

This project is one Commander player with an idea, a lot of testing, and AI tools helping with the heavy lifting.

Because of that, these examples should be read honestly: they are not polished commercial case studies. They are practical snapshots of a developing community tool being tested deck by deck.

## Why I Started Building This

The Dragon’s Touch started because I love Commander deckbuilding, but I was spending more time preparing to play than actually playing.

I would spend hours researching cards, sorting through my collection, comparing possible upgrades, and trying to figure out which owned cards actually fit a deck. Between being a Field Artillery Officer, a dad, and someone with limited hobby time, I wanted to reduce the busywork around deckbuilding without removing the creativity, theme, or final decision-making that makes Commander fun.

The goal is not to make decks for people.

The goal is to help players spend less time buried in sorting, searching, and second-guessing, and more time actually playing the game.

## How to Read These Examples

Each example folder shows one deck review workflow.

The examples may include:

- The original decklist
- A short showcase summary
- Suggested test swaps
- Excerpts from the generated deck report
- A guided review summary
- Screenshots or cropped excerpts showing how the tool handled the deck

The suggested swaps are not presented as perfect answers. They are test packages for the pilot to consider, playtest, reject, or adjust.

The player still makes the final decision.

### Example 001 — Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd: “Corgi Butt”

A legal 100-card Commander deck using Phelia, Exuberant Shepherd as a Dog/corgi typal flicker-control and token-combat commander.

This example shows how The Dragon’s Touch handled an optional tuning review with:

- No required cuts
- Collection-only recommendations
- Theme / Vibe philosophy lens
- Bethany, The Theme Mentor guide/persona
- A corrected strategy read from the pilot
- Optional test swaps instead of mandatory optimization
- Protection for theme-critical and commander-critical cards

Suggested test package:

Out:
- Soul Tithe
- Divine Gambit
- Sisay’s Ring

In:
- Hanged Executioner
- Odric, Lunarch Marshal
- Ajani, Caller of the Pride

These swaps are suggestions for testing, not final commands.
